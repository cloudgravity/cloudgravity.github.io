addresses the following challenges: (i) the increase in the number and diversity of objects to be supervised, (ii) the complexity of systems interconnected to form large critical infrastructures on a European scale, (iii) the existence of increasingly complex and silent targeted attacks, and (iv) the treatment of massive attacks which rapidly affect a significant number of victims.  Faced with these challenges, it is necessary to significantly improve the efficiency of the detection-reaction chain (response and remediation). The main objective of the project is therefore to provide new solutions and to advance the current scientific state of the art.

Main tasks included the generation of causality graphs in distributed systems, the development of explainable visualizations for IDSes, as well as the generation of test traffic for their evaluation.

CERES

Cybersecurity Evaluation in Realistic EnvironmentS

aims at building an attack and defense demonstrator in the domain of smart buildings, including numerous connected systems, subject to attacks attempting to disturb the building operation. Our tasks include (i) the specifications of expected security properties by defining security policies able to completely encompass the identified risks; (ii) the deployment of enforcement mechanisms for these extended security policies; (iii) the verification of the efficiency of these mechanisms by performing a rigourous and reproducible methodology; and (iv) the development of new detection and mitigation tools to combat attacks against smart buildings.

  • Partners: Télécom SudParis, Télécom Paris
